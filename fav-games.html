Arkham Horror: the Card Game

Arkham Horror: the Card Game is the prototypical living card game (regular release cycle of new content). It is best thought of as a boardgame made of cards. Very similar to a role-playing game, your deck of cards acts as your charaacter with different items, abilities, and actions. The theme of the game is dark, and sees you often failing. But don't worry, losing is fun, as they say. The game constantly pushes the frontier of narrative games with emergent story. There is a ton of content for this game and Fantasy Flight Games does not appear to be slowing down.

Heat: Pedal to the Metal

Heat is a racing game that truly feels fast. You play as a racecar driver playing cards from your to race around the track. The teach can be intimidating as each turn has many steps, but after one lap you are sure to get the hang of it. Winner of the Dice Towers "Game of the Year" award for 2022, it is engaging for all levels of gamer. The game comes with tournament mode, advanced weather cards, parts for racecar customization, and rules for "AI" players. One downside of the production is that the box leaves room for two more player pieces which are not included. To be able to play with eight people, you will need to purchase both upcoming expansions.

Earthborne Rangers

Earthborne Rangers is the first game from brand new publisher Earthborne Games. It had a successful kickstarter and a very successful Gamefound campaign for its second printing and new expansions. It is an open world adventure game in the style, but not theme, of Arkhom Horror: the Card Game. The team behind it even includes former employees (in particular former Head of Studio Andrew Navarro) from Fantasy Flight Games. The team knocked it completely out of the park with this optimistic view of the future. They have even managed to snag MJ Newman (codesigner for Arkham Horror: the Card Game). I can't wait to see what is in store for us.
